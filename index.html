Who am I?

I graduated from Haverford College with a double major in computer science and religon (more specifically religion's influence on modern social ethics). I chose computer science because of it's unique need for both strict logic and artistic creativity, and to this day it is what keeps me excited about the field. Religion, is shockingly similar, its foundation is ethical rather than logical, yet it inspires a similar need for creativity and imagination in order to craft arguments for how we should or can live together as humans. My thesis in religion used Black feminist theory and queer theory to develop a new and more holistic understanding of the social practice of covering, and my thesis in computer science focused on video games designed to treat/prevent dyslexia. In my free time I like to read (primarily black feminist queer and abolitionist literature), lift, play basketball, teach, and go to drag shows.

Lighthouse

Winner @ Philly Codefest 2023

Power Outage Hub

The power outage hub was designed for a client of IBM, the goal of the project was to improve the client's customers experience. I built out a django webapp with internal and external facing dashboards. The client facing (external) dashboard consisted of saftey informaiton, a radar, and a map of outages in the clients area. November 2022

SiFT: Simple File Transfer

SiFT v1.0 is server/client file transfer system with end to end encryption (incluing integrity and replay protection). The system was built out entirly in Python using the PyCryptodome library for AES and RSA encryption. The system has 5 protocols: login, commands, upload, download, and message transfer. The message transfer protocol uses AES in GCM mode for encryption. The AES key is created by encrypting a temporary key with RSA-OAEP.
